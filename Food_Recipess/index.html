Chicken Adobo

This is a popular Filipino dish made with chicken, soy sauce, and vinegar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or pork, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken or pork, soy sauce, and garlic. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the marinated meat and cook until browned.
  3. Add the onions and cook until softened.
  4. Pour in the vinegar and water. Add the bay leaves and peppercorns. Bring to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Garlic Fried Rice (Sinangag)

This is a simple and delicious Filipino fried rice dish made with garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cooked rice, preferably day-old
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped green onions (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook until golden brown and fragrant.
  3. Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps with a spatula.
  4. Stir-fry the rice, mixing well to ensure the garlic is evenly distributed.
  5. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions, if desired.
  8. Serve hot as a side dish or enjoy on its own.

Lechon

Lechon is a famous Filipino dish consisting of a whole roasted pig.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole pig (about 20-30 kg), cleaned and gutted
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 5 stalks lemongrass, pounded
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Banana leaves (for wrapping)
  • Charcoal (for roasting)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onion, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Rub the marinade all over the pig, both inside and out. Stuff the cavity with lemongrass.
  3. Wrap the pig in banana leaves and let it marinate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  4. Prepare the charcoal for roasting. Once the coals are hot, place the pig on a spit and secure it tightly.
  5. Roast the pig over the hot coals, turning it regularly to ensure even cooking. Baste occasionally with the remaining marinade.
  6. Continue roasting for about 4-5 hours, or until the skin is crispy and the meat is cooked through.
  7. Remove the pig from the spit and let it rest for a few minutes before carving.
  8. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Pancit

Pancit is a traditional Filipino noodle dish made with vegetables and meat.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 10 minutes or until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the garlic and onion, and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook until pink. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Add the chicken and cook until heated through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  5. Add the cabbage and carrot to the pan and stir-fry for a few minutes until tender.
  6. Return the shrimp and chicken to the pan. Add the soy sauce, fish sauce, and chicken broth. Bring to a boil.
  7. Add the rice noodles and toss to combine. Cook for a few minutes until the noodles are heated through and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert made with crushed ice, sweetened fruits, and milk.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shaved ice
  • 1/4 cup sweetened red beans
  • 1/4 cup sweetened white beans
  • 1/4 cup sweetened jackfruit
  • 1/4 cup sweetened banana
  • 1/4 cup sweetened coconut strips
  • 1/4 cup sweetened palm fruit
  • 1/4 cup sweetened purple yam
  • 1/4 cup sweetened macapuno (coconut sport)
  • 1/4 cup leche flan (caramel custard)
  • 1/4 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 scoop of ube ice cream

Instructions:

  1. In a tall glass, layer the sweetened red beans, white beans, jackfruit, banana, coconut strips, palm fruit, purple yam, and macapuno.
  2. Add the shaved ice on top of the layered ingredients.
  3. Pour the evaporated milk over the shaved ice.
  4. Top with a scoop of ube ice cream and a slice of leche flan.
  5. Serve immediately and mix well before eating.

Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew made with oxtail, vegetables, and a peanut sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup ground toasted rice
  • 1 bunch bok choy, chopped
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 banana heart, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp annatto oil
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shrimp paste (bagoong) for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail in water until tender. Remove the oxtail and set aside, reserving the broth.
  2. In a separate pan, heat the annatto oil over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add the oxtail to the pan and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in the reserved broth and bring to a boil.
  5. Add the peanut butter and ground toasted rice, stirring until well combined.
  6. Add the eggplant, banana heart, and bok choy. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with shrimp paste on the side.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a Filipino soup or stew characterized by its sour and savory taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly or ribs, cut into pieces
  • 1 packet sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 2-3 green chili peppers
  • 6 cups water
  • Salt and fish sauce to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil. Add the pork and cook until tender.
  2. Add the onion and tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  3. Add the sinigang mix and stir until dissolved.
  4. Add the radish, eggplant, and green chili peppers. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add the kangkong or spinach and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and fish sauce to taste.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a spicy Filipino dish made with pork, coconut milk, and chili peppers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 5-6 green chili peppers, sliced
  • 2-3 red chili peppers, sliced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and onion, and cook until fragrant.
  2. Add the pork belly and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until the pork is tender.
  5. Add the coconut cream and chili peppers. Simmer for another 10 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Caldereta

Caldereta is a Filipino meat stew made with beef, tomatoes, and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup liver spread
  • 1 large potato, cubed
  • 1 carrot, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 cup green peas (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Pour in the beef broth and tomato sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 1 hour or until the beef is tender.
  4. Add the liver spread and stir until well combined.
  5. Add the potato, carrot, red bell pepper, and green bell pepper. Cook for another 10-15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the grated cheese and green peas, if using, and stir until the cheese is melted.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Lumpia

Lumpia is a Filipino spring roll filled with vegetables and meat.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kg ground pork
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped carrots
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped green beans
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, carrots, onions, green beans, cabbage, garlic, egg, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Place a spoonful of the mixture onto a spring roll wrapper. Fold the sides and roll tightly, sealing the edge with a bit of water.
  3. Repeat with the remaining mixture and wrappers.
  4. Heat the oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the lumpia in batches until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Tocino

Tocino is a Filipino cured meat typically served for breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly, sliced thinly
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup banana ketchup
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp red food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the pineapple juice, brown sugar, soy sauce, banana ketchup, garlic, salt, black pepper, and red food coloring (if using). Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Add the pork belly slices to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 24 hours, preferably 2-3 days for best results.
  3. Heat a pan over medium heat. Add the marinated pork belly slices along with some of the marinade.
  4. Cook the pork, turning occasionally, until it is caramelized and cooked through, about 10-15 minutes.
  5. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy.

Sisig

Sisig is a Filipino dish made from parts of pig head and liver, usually seasoned with calamansi and chili peppers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork face (maskara), boiled and chopped
  • 1/2 kg pork liver, boiled and chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 green chili peppers, chopped
  • 2-3 red chili peppers, chopped
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped pork face and liver. Cook until slightly crispy.
  3. Add the green and red chili peppers. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Pour in the calamansi juice and soy sauce. Stir well to combine.
  5. Add the mayonnaise and mix until well incorporated.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, optionally on a sizzling plate, with steamed rice.

Bulalo

Bulalo is a Filipino beef marrow stew made with shank and bone marrow.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef shank with bone marrow
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2-3 ears of corn, cut into pieces
  • 1 bunch of pechay (bok choy)
  • 1 small cabbage, quartered
  • 2-3 potatoes, quartered
  • 2-3 saba bananas, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water
  • Fish sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the beef shank and onion. Simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until the meat is tender.
  2. Add the corn and cook for another 10 minutes.
  3. Add the potatoes and saba bananas. Cook until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add fish sauce if desired.
  5. Add the pechay and cabbage. Cook for another 2-3 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy.

Tinola

Tinola is a Filipino chicken soup usually served with green papaya and chili leaves.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 green papaya, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bunch chili leaves (dahon ng sili)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are lightly browned.
  3. Pour in the water and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer until the chicken is cooked through.
  4. Add the green papaya slices and cook until they are tender.
  5. Stir in the fish sauce and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Add the chili leaves and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Dinuguan

Dinuguan is a Filipino savory stew made of pork offal and blood.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork intestines and other pork offal, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 1 cup pork blood
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 2-3 green chili peppers, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the pork intestines and other offal, and cook until browned.
  3. Pour in the water and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer until the offal is tender.
  4. Add the vinegar and cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Slowly pour in the pork blood while stirring continuously to prevent curdling.
  6. Add the green chili peppers and simmer for another 10 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a Filipino vegetable dish made with mixed vegetables and shrimp paste.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kg pork belly, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup squash, cubed
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 cup okra, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pan over medium heat. Add the pork belly and cook until browned and crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, sauté the garlic, onion, and tomatoes until fragrant and softened.
  3. Add the shrimp paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Return the pork belly to the pan and mix well.
  5. Add the water and bring to a boil.
  6. Add the squash, eggplant, bitter melon, okra, and string beans. Stir to combine.
  7. Lower the heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Bibingka

Bibingka is a Filipino rice cake traditionally served during Christmas season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • Banana leaves (for lining)
  • Grated coconut (for topping)
  • Salted egg slices (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking pan with banana leaves and grease lightly with butter.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, eggs, and melted butter.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until well combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan.
  6. Top with grated coconut and salted egg slices.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving.

Puto

Puto is a Filipino steamed rice cake often served as a snack or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add the water to the dry ingredients, stirring until smooth and well combined.
  3. Pour the batter into small, greased molds or muffin cups, filling them about 3/4 full.
  4. Top with grated cheese if desired.
  5. Steam the puto over medium heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Remove from the steamer and let them cool slightly before removing from the molds.
  7. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Chicken Inasal

Chicken Inasal is a popular Filipino grilled chicken dish marinated in a mixture of calamansi, vinegar, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken thighs and drumsticks
  • 1/2 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup annatto oil
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the calamansi juice, vinegar, soy sauce, annatto oil, garlic, ginger, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken, basting occasionally with the marinade, until cooked through and slightly charred, about 20-30 minutes.
  4. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy.

Leche Flan

Leche Flan is a rich and creamy Filipino custard dessert topped with caramel syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 can (300ml) condensed milk
  • 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk
  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a pan over low heat, melt the sugar until it turns into a golden caramel. Pour it into a llanera (flan mold) and let it cool.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture to remove lumps, then pour it into the caramel-coated mold.
  4. Cover the mold with foil and steam for 30-40 minutes or until set.
  5. Cool completely before inverting onto a plate. Serve and enjoy!

Mango Float

Mango Float is a no-bake Filipino dessert known for its creamy texture and sweet mango layers.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe mangoes, sliced thinly
  • 2 packs graham crackers
  • 2 cups all-purpose cream (chilled)
  • 1 can (300ml) condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup crushed graham crackers (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the chilled all-purpose cream and condensed milk. Whip until well blended.
  2. In a rectangular dish, arrange a layer of graham crackers at the bottom.
  3. Spread a layer of the cream mixture over the crackers, then add sliced mangoes on top.
  4. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a cream layer.
  5. Sprinkle crushed graham crackers on top for garnish.
  6. Chill for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results.
  7. Slice, serve, and enjoy!

Maja Blanca

Maja Blanca is a traditional Filipino dessert made from coconut milk, corn, and sweet corn kernels.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1 cup fresh milk
  • 1 can (300ml) condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup sweet corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, chopped (for topping)
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (optional, for added flavor)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine coconut milk, fresh milk, condensed milk, and sugar. Stir well over medium heat.
  2. Slowly add cornstarch while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  3. Once the mixture thickens, add sweet corn kernels and stir for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased mold or tray and smooth the top.
  5. Sprinkle roasted peanuts and grated cheese on top for added flavor.
  6. Let it cool and set for at least 1 hour. Slice, serve, and enjoy!

Beef Batchoy

Beef Batchoy is a hearty Filipino noodle soup with tender beef cuts, egg noodles, and flavorful broth.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef brisket, thinly sliced
  • 200g beef liver, sliced thinly
  • 500g egg noodles (miki noodles)
  • 6 cups beef broth
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Chicharon (crushed) for topping
  • Spring onions (chopped) for garnish
  • Egg (optional, poached or boiled)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onions until fragrant.
  2. Add the beef brisket and cook until browned. Add beef liver and cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Pour in the beef broth, fish sauce, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 30 minutes until the beef is tender.
  4. Add the egg noodles and cook for another 3-5 minutes.
  5. Serve hot, topped with crushed chicharon, spring onions, and optional egg. Enjoy!

Crispy Pata

A crispy and tender Filipino pork dish served with soy-vinegar dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole pork leg (pata), cleaned
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp peppercorns
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • Water (enough to cover the pork leg)
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 1-2 red chilies, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Clean the pork leg thoroughly under running water.
  2. In a large pot, boil the pork leg with salt, peppercorns, garlic, bay leaves, and onion for 1.5 to 2 hours or until tender.
  3. Drain and let cool completely. Pat dry with paper towels.
  4. For best results, chill the pork leg in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours.
  5. Deep-fry the pork leg in hot oil until crispy and golden brown.
  6. For the dipping sauce, mix soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, garlic, onion, and chilies in a bowl.
  7. Chop the crispy pata into serving portions and serve with the dipping sauce. Enjoy!

Biko

Biko is a popular Filipino sticky rice cake dessert, perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • 4 cups coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup latik (coconut curds) for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the glutinous rice thoroughly and cook it in 2 cups of coconut milk with a pinch of salt. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  2. In a separate pan, combine the remaining coconut milk and brown sugar. Cook over medium heat until the mixture thickens and becomes syrupy.
  3. Once the rice is fully cooked, pour the sweet coconut mixture over the rice and stir well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a greased pan, press it down evenly, and garnish with latik if desired.
  5. Let it cool before slicing and serving. Enjoy!

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a traditional Filipino raw seafood dish marinated in vinegar and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh fish fillet (tuna, sardines, or mackerel), cubed
  • 1 cup vinegar (preferably cane or coconut vinegar)
  • 1/4 cup calamansi or lime juice
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 chili peppers, finely chopped (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 cup tomatoes, diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: coconut cream for added richness

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the fish cubes thoroughly in cold water and drain well.
  2. In a bowl, combine the fish, vinegar, and calamansi juice. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to "cook" the fish.
  3. Drain excess vinegar from the fish mixture.
  4. Add onions, chili, ginger, cucumber, and tomatoes. Mix well.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Optionally, add coconut cream for a richer flavor.
  6. Chill for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  7. Serve cold as an appetizer or with steamed rice.

Fish Escabeche

A delicious sweet and sour Filipino fish dish topped with sautéed vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg whole fish (like tilapia or lapu-lapu), cleaned and scored
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced thinly
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch (dissolved in water)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the fish with salt and pepper. Fry until golden brown, then set aside.
  2. In a separate pan, sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in oil until fragrant.
  3. Add the carrots and bell peppers. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Pour in vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, and water. Stir well.
  5. Let it simmer for 5 minutes, then add the cornstarch mixture to thicken the sauce.
  6. Place the fried fish on a serving platter and pour the sauce over it.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy with steamed rice!
